Tourist Destinations

Nara Park

Nara Park is a large park in central Nara. It is also home to hundreds of freely roaming deer.

Osaka Castle

Osaka Castle is one of the three great castles in Japan and affectionally called ”Mr. Taiko's castle” by the citizens of Osaka.

Mt. Fuji

Mount Fuji is with 3776 meters Japan's highest mountain. Mount Fuji is one of Japan's "Three Holy Mountains" along with Mount Tate and Mount Haku.

Universal Studios Japan

Universal Studios Japan (USJ) was the first theme park under the Universal Studios brand sto be built in Asia.

Fushimi Inari Taisha

Fushimi Inari Shrine is an important Shinto shrine in southern Kyoto. It is famous for thousands of vermilion torii gates.

Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

Shinjuku Gyoen is one of Tokyo's largest and most popular parks. In spring Shinjuku Gyoen becomes one of the best places in the city to see cherry blossoms.

Philosopher's Walk

The Philosopher's Path is a pleasant stone path through the northern part of Kyoto's Higashiyama district.

Himeji Castle

Himeji Castle, also known as White Heron Castle due to its elegant, white appearance, is widely considered as Japan's most spectacular castle.

Zenkoji Temple

Nagano's Zenkoji Temple is one of the most important and popular temples in Japan

Hakone Open-Air Museum

The Hakone Open Air Museum successfully attempts to create a harmonic balance of nature and art by exhibiting various sculptures on its grounds.
